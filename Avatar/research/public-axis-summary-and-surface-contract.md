# 🪞 Public Axis Summary and Surface Contract

> A visible surface is not the same thing as a legal one.  
> In WFGY 5.0 Avatar, public-axis summary and surface contract exist so public-facing visibility can remain helpful, bounded, and readable without pretending to be evidence, authority, or completion.

**Quick links:** [Research Hub](./README.md) · [Protocol Layer and Control Precedence](./protocol-layer-and-control-precedence.md) · [Master Toggle Map and Central TXT Toggle Block](./master-toggle-map-and-central-txt-toggle-block.md) · [Output Governance Core](./output-governance-core.md) · [Return-Path Rebind Controls](./return-path-rebind-controls.md) · [Architecture Overview](./architecture-overview.md) · [Blackfan Testing](../eval/blackfan-testing.md)

## 🧭 Why this page exists

A public-facing surface is one of the easiest places to fake maturity.

A system shows a compact summary.
A system exposes a visible surface contract.
A system becomes easier to read.
A system starts looking more structured and more intentional.

And then a weak reader starts telling a dangerous story:

1. a visible summary must mean the underlying reasoning is safe
2. a stronger surface contract must mean stronger authority
3. a cleaner public axis must mean better evidence
4. if the output explains its own surface well, the deeper law probably no longer matters
5. if a summary is compact and confident, completion is probably close enough to imply

The packed master rejects that story.

Public-axis summary and surface contract exist because public-facing visibility is useful.
They also exist because public-facing visibility is easy to overtrust.
This page exists to stop that overtrust.

## 🧩 What public_axis_summary actually is

`public_axis_summary` is the bounded field that governs whether a compact public-facing axis summary appears and, if it appears, how much public-visible axis framing is exposed.

The packed master preserves:

1. `off`
2. `compact`

This matters because the system does **not** preserve an unrestricted summary ladder here.
Even at schema level, the field is already bounded.

That tells you something important:

public-axis summary is meant for bounded orientation, not for replacing the answer body and not for turning internal structure into a public scoreboard.

---

## 🚫 Public-axis summary is not evidence

This boundary has to be stated directly.

A public-axis summary may help the reader see:

1. what kind of answer posture is active
2. what kind of public-facing organization is being used
3. what kind of bounded directional framing is present

It may **not** prove:

1. support strength
2. legality
3. authority
4. final acceptance
5. theorem-facing closure

This matters because compact summaries are persuasive.
A small well-phrased summary can make readers believe the deeper structure has already been earned.
The packed master explicitly blocks that leap.

---

## 🧭 Why public-axis summary remains bounded

The schema keeps `public_axis_summary` to `off | compact` for a reason.

That reason is not weakness.
It is honesty.

If the field were allowed to become an unrestricted presentation engine, several failures would become easy:

1. surface framing would start replacing deeper law
2. compact summaries would start functioning like legitimacy badges
3. public orientation would start pretending to be internal evidence
4. answer posture would be over-compressed into highly confident surface shorthand

So boundedness here is not a limitation accident.
It is part of the law.

## 🪞 What surface_contract actually is

`surface_contract` is the bounded field that governs how explicitly the answer presents its public-facing surface agreement with the reader.

The packed master preserves:

1. `minimal`
2. `visible`
3. `strong`

This matters because surface contract is not just layout.
It governs how much the answer openly exposes its public-facing structure, pacing, and contract with the user at the surface layer.

That includes things like:

1. how visible the answer’s organization is
2. how strongly the public-facing posture is signaled
3. how explicit the answer is about its own bounded surface stance

This is real.
But it is still bounded.

---

## 🚫 Surface contract is not authority contract

This is the page’s second hard boundary.

Surface contract is **not**:

1. authority contract
2. evidence contract
3. permission contract
4. acceptance contract

A stronger surface contract may make the answer feel:

1. more visible
2. more deliberate
3. more stable
4. more public-facing

It may **not** make the answer more:

1. supported
2. legally justified
3. final
4. sovereign

This matters because strong visible surface behavior is one of the easiest counterfeiters of deeper readiness.

The packed master explicitly keeps those layers apart.

---

## 🧷 minimal, visible, and strong are not power levels

The three surface-contract states are easy to misread as stronger and weaker versions of truth.

That is wrong.

They are bounded visibility modes, not authority ranks.

That means:

1. `minimal` does not mean less legal
2. `visible` does not mean more supported
3. `strong` does not mean more final

Instead, they describe how much public-facing surface contract is made explicit.

This matters because otherwise the whole field becomes a prestige slider.
The packed master explicitly blocks that mistake.

---

## 🌫️ minimal

`minimal` preserves a thinner public-facing surface contract.

That may lawfully support:

1. lighter visible framing
2. reduced surface emphasis
3. lower explicitness in public-facing contract cues

It may **not** imply:

1. weaker legality
2. weaker evidence
3. weaker controller correctness
4. lower truth status

Minimal is a visibility choice inside law, not a reduction of law itself.

---

## 👁️ visible

`visible` preserves a clear but bounded public-facing surface contract.

That may lawfully support:

1. readable structure
2. explicit public-facing organization
3. stable answer-shape visibility
4. bounded orientation cues

It may **not** imply:

1. stronger support
2. stronger permission
3. stronger maturity by itself
4. stronger finality

Visible is often the safest ordinary-use posture because it helps readability without trying to overperform authority.

## 🔁 Public-axis summary and surface contract are related, not identical

These two families often appear near each other, but they do different work.

`public_axis_summary` governs:
1. whether a compact public-facing axis summary appears

`surface_contract` governs:
1. how explicitly the public-facing surface agreement is shown

That means one is more summary-facing, and the other is more contract-facing.

A weak system blurs them and starts talking vaguely about “surface clarity.”
The packed master keeps them distinct because summary and contract can drift differently:

1. a summary can be compact while the surface contract stays minimal
2. the surface contract can be strong while the summary remains off
3. both can be present without becoming authority signals

That distinction matters a lot.
